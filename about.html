About Studio Ghibli

Discover the story behind the legendary animation studio known for films like Spirited Away, My Neighbor Totoro, and Howl’s Moving Castle.

Our Origins

Studio Ghibli was founded in 1985 by renowned directors Hayao Miyazaki and Isao Takahata after the success of Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind. Since then, the studio has become a cultural touchstone in animation, captivating audiences worldwide with powerful storytelling and hand-drawn artistry.

Philosophy & Vision

Studio Ghibli films often explore themes of environmentalism, pacifism, feminism, and childhood wonder. These stories are infused with warmth, emotion, and a respect for both nature and humanity. Each frame is carefully crafted to evoke emotion and immerse viewers in fantastical worlds grounded in realism.

Legacy

The studio earned international acclaim when Spirited Away won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature in 2003. Today, Studio Ghibli remains one of the most respected animation houses in the world, beloved for its emotional depth and iconic characters.
